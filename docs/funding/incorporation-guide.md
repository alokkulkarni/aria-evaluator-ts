# ARIA Evaluator — Incorporation Guidance

## Delaware C-Corp vs UK Limited Company

| Factor | Delaware C-Corp | UK Limited Company |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| VC preference | Strong (US VCs require it) | Fine for EU VCs |
| Cost | ~$500 + $300/yr franchise tax | ~£50 + £13/yr |
| Tax | 21% federal + state | 19-25% corp tax |
| ESOP | Straightforward (ISOs) | EMI scheme (tax-efficient) |
| IP protection | Strong | Strong |
| Recommended if | Targeting US VCs | Targeting EU VCs, based in UK |

## Practical recommendation

For **ARIA Evaluator**, the right structure depends mostly on fundraising target:

- Choose **Delaware C-Corp** if the company intends to raise from **US seed funds, US angels, or global VCs that default to Delaware structures**
- Choose **UK Ltd** if the founders are UK-based, expect to hire in the UK first, and plan to raise primarily from **UK / EU investors** before any US institutional round

If the financing strategy is explicitly **US VC-first**, forming as a **Delaware C-Corp** now is usually the cleanest path.

## Steps for Delaware C-Corp

1. **Incorporate via Stripe Atlas ($500) or Clerky ($799)**  
   Both are standard for venture-backed startups. Stripe Atlas is simpler; Clerky is often preferred once counsel is involved.

2. **Issue founder shares (83(b) election within 30 days!)**  
   Set founder stock purchase agreements, vesting schedules, and board approvals immediately.

3. **Set up registered agent (included with Stripe Atlas)**  
   Ensure the company has a Delaware registered agent from day one.

4. **Open business bank account (Mercury or SVB)**  
   Use a dedicated business account before taking investment funds or customer revenue.

5. **Get EIN from IRS**  
   Required for banking, payroll, and tax setup.

6. **File initial franchise tax return**  
   Track annual Delaware filing obligations so the company does not fall out of good standing.

### Delaware add-ons worth doing immediately

- Adopt **IP assignment agreements** for all founders and contractors
- Approve **founder vesting** and board consents
- Set up a lightweight cap table system such as **Carta** or **Pulley**
- Put standard contractor and confidentiality agreements in place before external development work

## Steps for UK Ltd

1. **Register at Companies House (£50 online)**  
   Form the company and appoint directors.

2. **Issue founder shares**  
   Document allotments clearly and maintain the company register from the start.

3. **Register for Corporation Tax with HMRC**  
   Complete tax registration promptly after incorporation.

4. **Open business bank account (Tide, Revolut Business)**  
   Separate company and personal funds immediately.

5. **Register for VAT if revenue > £90K threshold**  
   Even before the threshold, some companies voluntarily register depending on customer profile.

### UK add-ons worth doing immediately

- Put **founder service agreements** in place
- Execute **IP assignments** from founders and contractors into the company
- Explore **EMI option scheme** setup once the first employees are hired
- Confirm data protection responsibilities if handling customer or employee data in the UK / EU

## Decision framework for ARIA Evaluator

### Choose Delaware C-Corp if:

- primary target investors are US funds
- you want the simplest path to SAFEs, preferred stock rounds, and US legal norms
- the company expects US enterprise customers or US hiring early
- a future top-tier seed / Series A round is a near-term goal

### Choose UK Ltd if:

- founders and early employees are in the UK
- early fundraising is likely to come from angels, UK micro-VCs, or grant programs
- the team wants lower setup and maintenance cost initially
- the business may remain UK-centric for the first phase

## Common mistakes to avoid

- Delaying **83(b)** filings after founder stock issuance
- Leaving IP in individual founder names instead of the company
- Hiring contractors before paperwork is in place
- Mixing personal and company expenses
- Waiting too long to formalize vesting, stock records, and board approvals

## Bottom line

- **US VC target:** form a **Delaware C-Corp**
- **UK / EU-first target with lower admin cost:** start as a **UK Ltd**

If ARIA Evaluator is positioning for a US-style seed round with SAFEs and global institutional investors, **Delaware C-Corp is the default recommendation**.
