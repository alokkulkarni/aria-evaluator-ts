# Data Processing Agreement (Template)

- **Effective date:** June 2026
- **Version:** 1.0
- **Owner:** Founder / Security Lead
- **Use case:** Enterprise customer contracting template

## 1. Parties

This Data Processing Agreement ("DPA") is entered into between:

- **Customer** (the "Controller")
- **AriaEval Ltd, trading as ARIA Evaluator** (the "Processor")

This DPA supplements the parties' master services agreement, subscription agreement, or order form.

## 2. Definitions

For purposes of this DPA:

- **Controller** means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data.
- **Processor** means the entity that processes personal data on behalf of the controller.
- **Personal Data** means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person.
- **Processing** means any operation performed on personal data, including collection, storage, use, disclosure, or deletion.

## 3. Subject Matter and Duration

The subject matter of the processing is ARIA Evaluator's provision of AI evaluation services to Customer. Processing will continue for the term of the applicable services agreement and for any limited post-termination period required to return or delete data, comply with law, or complete agreed transition activities.

## 4. Nature and Purpose of Processing

Processor will process personal data only as necessary to:

- Provide ARIA Evaluator services
- Execute customer-requested AI evaluations
- Store evaluation results and related metadata for the agreed retention period
- Secure, monitor, support, and troubleshoot the service
- Comply with applicable legal and contractual obligations

## 5. Types of Personal Data

The categories of personal data processed under this DPA may include:

- Evaluation prompts and responses
- Evaluation scores and rubrics
- Run metadata and operational logs
- Account identifiers and support correspondence supplied by Customer

## 6. Categories of Data Subjects

The categories of data subjects may include:

- Customer's end users
- Customer's employees, contractors, or authorized users

## 7. Processor Obligations

AriaEval will:

1. Process personal data only on documented instructions from Customer, unless required by law.
2. Implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures appropriate to the risk.
3. Ensure personnel with access to personal data are bound by confidentiality obligations.
4. Notify Customer of any intended new sub-processor that materially affects service delivery and give at least reasonable advance notice.
5. Notify Customer of a confirmed personal data breach affecting Customer data **without undue delay and, where feasible, within 48 hours of confirmation**.
6. Provide reasonable assistance with data subject requests, DPIAs, transfer assessments, and supervisory authority inquiries, taking into account the nature of processing.
7. Delete or return Customer personal data upon termination, unless retention is required by applicable law.
8. Maintain records and information reasonably necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA.

## 8. Sub-Processors

Customer authorizes Processor to use the following categories of sub-processors for service delivery:

- **Amazon Web Services (AWS)** for infrastructure, storage, networking, and security services
- **AWS Bedrock and underlying LLM providers made available through Bedrock** for model inference and evaluation processing

Processor remains responsible for its sub-processors to the extent required by applicable law and will maintain written terms with sub-processors imposing data protection obligations appropriate to the services performed.

## 9. International Transfers

Where personal data is transferred outside the United Kingdom, EEA, or Switzerland, Processor will implement appropriate safeguards, including **Standard Contractual Clauses** or other lawful transfer mechanisms recognized by applicable law.

## 10. Audit Rights

Upon reasonable written notice and no more than once annually, Customer may request information reasonably necessary to verify Processor's compliance with this DPA. Where required by law or contract, the parties may agree on a scoped audit process that protects Processor's confidential information, platform security, and other customers.

## 11. Liability

Each party's liability under this DPA will be subject to the liability limitations and exclusions in the underlying services agreement, except to the extent such limitations are prohibited by applicable law.

## 12. Term and Termination

This DPA remains in effect for as long as Processor processes personal data on behalf of Customer under the services agreement. On termination or expiry of the services agreement, Processor will return or delete Customer personal data in accordance with the agreement, this DPA, and applicable law.
