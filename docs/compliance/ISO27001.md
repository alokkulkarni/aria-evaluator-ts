---

## What is ISO 27001?

ISO 27001 is the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS). It provides a systematic approach to managing sensitive information through policies, processes, and technical controls. Certification demonstrates to customers and regulators that your organization follows best practices for information security.

**Why we need it**: Many enterprise customers (especially EU-based) require ISO 27001 as a procurement prerequisite. It complements SOC 2 and strengthens our GDPR compliance posture.

## Certification Process

1. **Gap analysis** (4–6 weeks) — Compare current state against all 93 controls
2. **ISMS documentation** (6–8 weeks) — Policies, procedures, risk assessment
3. **Implementation** (8–12 weeks) — Close gaps, implement missing controls
4. **Internal audit** (2–4 weeks) — Self-assessment against the standard
5. **Stage 1 audit** (1 week) — Documentation review by certification body
6. **Stage 2 audit** (1–2 weeks) — Implementation verification by certification body
7. **Certification issued** — Valid for 3 years with annual surveillance audits

### Estimated Timeline: 6–9 months from initiation

### Estimated Cost

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Certification body (initial audit) | £15,000–30,000 |
| Consultant support (optional) | £10,000–25,000 |
| Annual surveillance audit | £5,000–10,000 |
| Re-certification (every 3 years) | £10,000–20,000 |

## Overlap with SOC 2

ISO 27001 and SOC 2 share approximately 70% of control requirements. Pursuing both in parallel significantly reduces duplicated effort. The main areas of overlap are:

| Area | ISO 27001 | SOC 2 |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Access control | A.5.15–A.5.18, A.8.2–A.8.5 | CC6.1–CC6.3 |
| Encryption | A.8.24 | CC6.7 |
| Logging & monitoring | A.8.15–A.8.16 | CC7.1–CC7.2 |
| Change management | A.8.32 | CC8.1 |
| Incident response | A.5.24–A.5.28 | CC7.3–CC7.4 |
| Vendor management | A.5.19–A.5.22 | CC9.1–CC9.2 |
| Risk assessment | (Clause 6.1) | CC3.1–CC3.4 |
