- **Effective date:** June 2026
- **Version:** 1.0
- **Owner:** Founder / Security Lead
- **Next review date:** December 2026

## Purpose

This document summarizes ARIA Evaluator’s privacy program in a practical format for customers, auditors, and internal stakeholders.

## Program Overview

ARIA Evaluator is building its privacy program to support early-stage SaaS operations while aligning with customer expectations and applicable privacy law. The current focus is on GDPR readiness, data minimization, secure processing, transparent disclosures, and practical user rights workflows.

## GDPR Compliance Status

ARIA Evaluator considers GDPR a priority framework and is working toward a documented, operationally sustainable compliance posture. Current controls include encryption, access controls, retention practices, incident response planning, cookie consent, and user rights workflows.

## Data Processing Register Summary

At a high level, ARIA Evaluator maintains records of the main categories of processing that support the service, including:

- Account and authentication data
- Evaluation data, prompts, outputs, and reports
- Audit and security logs
- Billing and commercial records
- Cookie and preference management data

These records are reviewed and expanded as the product and vendor footprint evolve.

## Data Subject Rights Implementation

ARIA Evaluator supports core user rights workflows through product and platform controls, including:

- **Data export:** `GET /api/auth/account/export`
- **Account deletion:** `DELETE /api/auth/account`
- **Cookie consent:** granular controls for consent categories

Privacy requests and questions may be directed to **privacy@ariaeval.io**.

## Privacy by Design Principles

ARIA Evaluator applies privacy by design through the following working principles:

- Collect only data needed to operate and improve the service
- Protect data in transit and at rest
- Limit access to people with a legitimate business need
- Define retention and deletion behavior as the platform matures
- Prefer designs that reduce unnecessary personal data exposure

## Enterprise and Processor Commitments

For enterprise customers and other customers requiring processor terms, ARIA Evaluator will support **Data Processing Agreements (DPAs)** that describe processing scope, security commitments, sub-processor use, and breach-notification obligations.

## Sub-Processor Transparency

ARIA Evaluator is committed to maintaining visibility into sub-processors used for core service delivery and to providing **sub-processor notification** commitments through customer contracting where required.

## Governance

The Founder / Security Lead owns the privacy program until dedicated privacy leadership is added. Privacy, security, and engineering decisions should be coordinated when product or architecture changes materially affect personal data processing.
