# Acceptable Use Policy

- **Effective date:** June 2026
- **Version:** 1.0
- **Owner:** Founder / Security Lead
- **Next review date:** December 2026

## Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy sets baseline expectations for employees and contractors using ARIA Evaluator systems, devices, accounts, and data.

## Scope

This policy applies to all company-managed systems, cloud environments, repositories, communication tools, endpoints used for company work, and any customer data accessed in the course of work.

## Permitted Use

Employees and contractors may use company systems to:

- Build, operate, support, and secure the ARIA Evaluator platform
- Communicate with customers, vendors, and teammates for legitimate business purposes
- Access company information needed for approved job responsibilities

Use must be lawful, ethical, and aligned with ARIA Evaluator’s business interests.

## Security Requirements

Users must:

- Use passwords with **12 or more characters** and do not reuse passwords from other services
- Keep credentials private and never share accounts or authentication tokens
- Use only approved methods to access company systems and customer data
- Report suspected phishing, malware, unauthorized access, or other security issues promptly
- Lock devices when unattended and apply required updates in a timely manner

## Customer Data Rules

- Customer data must be accessed only for legitimate business need
- Customer data must not be stored on personal devices unless explicitly approved and protected
- Customer data must not be copied into personal email, notes apps, or consumer AI tools
- Restricted data such as secrets and keys must be handled only through approved secure systems

## Prohibited Activities

The following are prohibited:

- Sharing passwords or bypassing security controls
- Accessing data or systems without authorization
- Using company systems for harassment, fraud, illegal activity, or abusive behavior
- Introducing unlicensed, unapproved, or malicious software into company environments
- Exporting, scraping, or retaining customer data beyond an approved business purpose

## Code of Ethics

ARIA Evaluator expects team members to act honestly, respect customer trust, protect privacy, and avoid actions that could cause harm to users, customers, coworkers, or the company. If something feels legally questionable, unsafe, or ethically wrong, stop and escalate it.

## Reporting

Security concerns, suspected misuse, or policy violations must be reported immediately to the Founder / Security Lead or to **security@ariaeval.io**.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in loss of access, disciplinary action, contract termination, or legal action, depending on severity.
